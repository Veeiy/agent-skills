Inputs used: mission brief, blueprint.md (market framing, audience, price framing, compliance lines), handoff schema.
Missing inputs: none. Brand tokens were "to be created in run," so they are defined below.

Compliance carried into this concept: no em dashes; product is fictional and staging only, so all copy stays inside a claim boundary. No copy implies a real operating service (no "trusted by X," no send counts, no uptime or security certifications, no real signup or payment). The CTA leads to a test-mode flow only.

---

## 1. Positioning line

For freelancers and small studios who send client deliverables, Ferry is the file-sharing link that expires on your terms, by time or by download count, so a one-off send never lives online forever.

---

## 2. Hero message

**Headline:** Send the file. Set the expiry. Done.

**Subhead:** Ferry gives your delivery a link that disappears after a set number of days or downloads, so client work does not sit on a server forever.

---

## 3. Value props

1. **Expires on your terms**
   Choose a time limit or a download count when you send. When the limit is hit, the link stops working.

2. **No account to grab it**
   Your client clicks the link and downloads. No sign-up, no app, no friction on their end.

3. **Clean and out of the way**
   Drop a file, get a link, share it. No folders to manage and no inbox to clean up later.

---

## 4. Primary CTA

**Action the page drives:** start a send (the single hero action; everything points to it).

**Button label:** Send a file

Secondary, lower on the page, same destination flow: "Try it" (for the pricing/free-tier block). Both route to the same test-mode send flow. No email capture, no payment.

---

## 5. Brand direction

**Tone (3 words):** calm, plain-spoken, dependable.

**Visual direction (minimal):**
- Primary color: deep teal `#0E7C7B` (the "ferry / water" cue, trustworthy without being generic SaaS blue).
- Accent: warm amber `#F2A541` (used only for the primary button and the expiry highlight, so the one action and the one differentiator pop).
- Neutrals: near-black text `#1A1A1A` on off-white `#FAFAF7`; light gray `#E6E6E1` for borders and dividers.
- Font feel: one clean geometric or humanist sans for everything (think Inter or similar). Bold weight for the headline, regular for body. No serif, no display font. Generous whitespace, single column, large hero.
- Motif: a simple expiry cue (a small countdown or "expires in" tag) shown near the link, since the expiry is the whole wedge. Keep it to one understated element, not a theme.

---

## 6. Riskiest assumption + cheap test

**Riskiest assumption:** that "the link expires" reads as a benefit (privacy, no clutter, control) rather than a drawback (fear of losing the file, client cannot re-download when they need it). If freelancers read expiry as risk instead of relief, the entire wedge inverts and the page fails.

**Cheapest test:** in the two persona passes already scheduled for this run, show the hero and ask one question before any explanation: "What does an expiring link make you feel, safer or nervous?" If a persona reads it as nervous, the fix is a reassurance line near the CTA (for example, that the sender controls the limit and can send again anytime) rather than a positioning change. This costs nothing extra; it rides on the persona tests in the test wave.

---

## Handoff notes for downstream builders

- Both deliverables (index.html and the one-pager) build from the exact strings above. Do not paraphrase the headline, subhead, value-prop labels, or button label; keep them identical across the page and the one-pager so the cross-consistency gate passes.
- Price framing to display (framing only, no checkout): Free up to 2GB with 7-day expiry; Pro at a flat low monthly for larger files and custom expiry. Show as a simple two-tier block, not a real purchase path.
- Keep the amber accent reserved for the primary button and the expiry tag only. If it shows up elsewhere the "one action, one differentiator" intent is lost.
