## Task Completion

**No.** The primary task (send a file and get a link) cannot be completed. The "Create link" button is disabled in test mode with no working alternative path. A user who clicks through to set an expiry expects to proceed to the next step (copy the link, send to client, or sign up); instead, they hit a wall. This is a blocker that kills the value prop mid-flow.

---

## Findings (severity-ranked)

1. **[BLOCKER] Disabled "Create link" button halts the primary flow.** The modal's main CTA is grayed out with a caption "demo, disabled." A visitor who engages with the expiry picker expects to either get a link they can copy or advance to signup. Instead, they're told the feature is off. This breaks the proof of concept and likely causes bounce. Either the button should launch a real signup flow, or it should display a copyable sample link (e.g., "Copy link: ferry.link/demo-7k2p9") to prove the flow works.

2. **[MAJOR] Muted text contrast fails WCAG AA.** The subheading ("Ferry gives your delivery a link..."), card descriptions, footer text, and modal subheading all use `color: #5a5a55` on `background: #FAFAF7`. Contrast ratio is approximately 2.5:1; AA compliance requires 4.5:1. This fails readability for users with moderate vision loss and violates WCAG 2.1 AA. Raise the muted color to approximately `#3d3d38` or darken the background.

3. **[MEDIUM] Demo disclaimer is buried and unclear.** The modal shows "This is a demo flow. Pick how the link should retire. Nothing is uploaded or sent." buried under the modal title. A faster reader (like the persona) may miss this and expect a real outcome. Move this disclaimer above the form or into a callout banner so it's unavoidable before interaction.

---

## Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)

- **Contrast:** Fail. Muted text (#5a5a55 on #FAFAF7) = ~2.5:1 ratio. Fails AA. Affects: subheading, card copy, footer, modal subtext.
- **Touch targets:** Pass. Buttons are 32px+ in height. Modal controls (mode toggle, chips) are clickable at 9px padding. Mobile may be tight below 760px breakpoint but still operable.
- **Keyboard reachability of primary flow:** Pass. All buttons tabable, Escape closes modal, focus not trapped. The "Send a file" button (CTA) is reachable via keyboard. The expiry picker and button navigation work with arrow keys and Tab.
- **Alt text / focus visibility:** Pass on alt text (icons are decorative emoji, no meaningful images). Focus visible: buttons use `:active` and `:hover` states; no focus indicator CSS visible in the markup, but browser default focus ring applies.

## Persona Verdict

**Would this user buy?** Not yet. The hero and value prop ("control when the link expires") hit exactly what a freelancer like me needs. I hate WeTransfer's forever links, and Ferry's promise to let me set a 3-day expiry is what would get me to sign up. But the modal's disabled button kills it. I set the expiry, I see the link preview, I'm ready to copy and send... and then nothing. I'd close the tab and go back to WeTransfer.

**One change that would move me from bounce to buy:** Make the "Create link" button active and show me a copy-to-clipboard flow (even if it's fake/demo text). Let me see what success feels like. Even a "Go to signup" redirect would work. Right now, the flow is a dead end, and I'm too busy to care about a half-baked demo.
