## 1. Product summary (positioning)

For freelancers and small studios who send client deliverables, Ferry is the file-sharing link that expires on your terms, by time or by download count, so a one-off send never lives online forever.

Hero line carried verbatim from the page: **Send the file. Set the expiry. Done.**

---

## 2. Target audience

**Who:** Solo freelancers and 2-to-5-person studios who hand finished work to clients. Designers, editors, photographers, copywriters, and small agencies. People who deliver a file, not a folder, and who do not want that file sitting on a server after the job is closed.

**The moment they reach for Ferry:** A project just wrapped. The final cut, the logo pack, the retouched gallery, or the signed deck is ready. They need to get it to the client right now, without making the client create an account, and without that download link floating around a shared drive for the next two years. That is the exact moment Ferry fits: one send, a limit they set, done.

---

## 3. Three launch channels

1. **Reddit (r/freelance, r/graphic_design, r/editors):** Post a short "I built a tiny tool for sending client files that expire" note in a relevant weekly self-promo or feedback thread. Angle: the privacy-and-no-clutter wedge, told as a freelancer's own annoyance, with a link to the demo, not a pitch.

2. **Indie Hackers (Product / "Show IH"):** Share the demo as a launch post focused on the one wedge, expiring links, and ask for feedback on whether expiry reads as relief or worry. Angle: founder-to-founder, lead with the riskiest assumption so the comments do the validation.

3. **X / Twitter (build-in-public + design-freelancer circles):** A short thread showing the send-then-expire flow in three steps. Angle: "client work should not live online forever," with the demo link and a one-line ask to try the test-mode send. Reply into freelancer and indie-maker threads where file delivery comes up.

## 4. Pre-launch checklist

- [ ] Landing page loads clean on desktop and mobile, single column, hero and the three value-prop blocks (Expires on your terms / No account to grab it / Clean and out of the way) all render.
- [ ] Headline, subhead, value-prop labels, and the "Send a file" button label match the concept word for word, no paraphrase.
- [ ] Pricing block shows the two tiers as framing only: Free up to 2GB with 7-day expiry, and Pro at a flat low monthly. Confirm NO dollar figure appears anywhere and there is no real checkout.
- [ ] Test-mode send flow works end to end: pick a file, set a time limit or a download count, get a link. No email capture, no payment, no real upload that persists.
- [ ] Reassurance near the CTA is in place (sender controls the limit and can send again anytime), so an expiring link reads as control, not loss.
- [ ] Demo is clearly marked as a staging/demo of a fictional product so no visitor mistakes it for a live service.
- [ ] A basic analytics and feedback hook is live: a privacy-light page-view counter plus one visible "send feedback" link or short form, so week-one signal is captured.
- [ ] The three launch posts are drafted, each leading with the expiring-link wedge (not a generic file-share pitch), and pointed at the demo link.

---

## 5. Week-one success metric

**Demo send-flow completion rate:** of visitors who land on the page, the share who start a send and reach a generated link in test mode. This is the one number that tells you whether the expiring-link wedge pulls people to act, rather than just reading and leaving. Target a directional read in week one; if completions are near zero, the wedge or the reassurance line is the thing to fix first.
