## 1. Where the personas agree, diverge, and how I weighed them

### Agreement (these are the real issues)
- **Contrast fails WCAG AA.** Both personas independently ran the a11y baseline and both found contrast failures. They landed on *different elements*, which strengthens rather than contradicts the finding:
  - Persona 1: `--muted` `#5a5a55` on `--bg` `#FAFAF7` (index.html line 25), affecting subhead (line 515), card copy (lines 535/540/545), footer, modal sub. ~2.5:1, needs 4.5:1.
  - Persona 2: `.expiry-tag` `#7a4d05` on `rgba(242,165,65,0.16)` (index.html lines 294/297). Below 4.5:1.
  - Verdict: contrast is a genuine multi-surface AA defect. Confirmed in the CSS. In-scope to fix (token/color change only).
- **Same bottom line:** both report task NOT completed and "would not buy" as-is.

### Divergence (weighed by severity)
- **Persona 1 BLOCKER is functional and universal.** The modal's primary CTA is `<button class="btn-disabled" disabled>Create link (demo, disabled)</button>` (index.html line 674). Every visitor who opens the modal and engages the expiry picker hits a dead end. A demo link string already exists one line up (`ferry.link/demo-7k2p9`, line 670). Highest-value fix, and cleanly in-scope: make the button active and reveal/copy the FAKE demo link. No real backend implied. **This is the load-bearing fix.**
- **Persona 2 BLOCKER is persona-specific trust *copy*, not a broken flow.** Asks for an explicit "files deleted in X days" SLA, a privacy policy link, and data-residency disclosure. Real for the privacy segment, but weighed *down* for two reasons:
  1. It is content, not a functional dead end — it costs interest, not the ability to complete the flow.
  2. Inventing a precise deletion SLA or a real privacy/GDPR/data-center claim for a **fictional** product manufactures an operational assertion the wave-1 concept never specified. A hard fabricated SLA is out-of-scope and a grounding risk.
  - In-scope version that captures most of the value: soften to qualitative, already-true reassurance ("files are removed when the link expires") and add a **demo-style placeholder** privacy link in the footer (clearly non-functional, consistent with the existing demo disclaimer). Fabricated data-residency / GDPR badge: **declined** as out-of-scope (would assert facts about a product that does not exist).
- **Keyboard focus gap (Persona 2, PARTIAL FAIL):** `.modal-close` (index.html lines 360–369) has no `:focus-visible` style. Confirmed — no focus rule exists in the CSS. Real AA keyboard defect, cheap and in-scope to fix. Folding it into the contrast pass since it is the same CSS surface.
- **"No account" claim (Persona 2, MEDIUM):** card line 539 says "No account to grab it"; body line 540 already clarifies "Your client clicks the link and downloads. No sign-up." The clarification is present. Mild, WARN-level, **not** routed this pass (do not gold-plate).
- Persona 2's MINOR reassurance-copy tweak is absorbed into the deletion-timeline softening above.
